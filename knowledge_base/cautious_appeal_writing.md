# Source label: cautious_appeal_writing.md

Write only what can be checked. State the notice reference, vehicle, date, location, allegation, short factual account, evidence list, requested outcome, and the official route used. Use "I believe", "the evidence shows", "please consider", and "please explain" where the point needs verification.

Do not invent a legal breach, quote a rule or deadline from memory, claim a fixed grace period, accuse an operator or council of fraud, say a notice is automatically invalid, promise a result, or say evidence exists when it has not been attached. Do not guess who drove, alter dates/times, or make a broad allegation without identifying the exact sign, image, payment, notice field or document involved.

Avoid generic defences pasted into every case. A short appeal tied to the evidence is stronger than a long list of unsupported points. If the notice is unclear, say which fact needs confirmation and ask the issuer to provide the relevant record or image.

ParkPal produces informational draft text only. Users must verify the official notice, route and deadline before submission.
