# Source label: council_pcn_overview.md

Council Penalty Charge Notices follow a different route from private parking charges. Identify the issuer, notice stage and stated deadline before responding; council instructions on the notice take priority for that case.

For many PCNs fixed to a vehicle, the first step is an informal challenge to the council. If it is rejected and a Notice to Owner (NtO) is later served, the vehicle owner can make formal representations. A postal PCN can sometimes act as the NtO, so it may require formal representations immediately rather than an informal challenge.

Common stages: PCN -> informal challenge where available -> Notice to Owner/formal representations -> Notice of Rejection -> independent adjudicator appeal. Do not treat a rejected informal challenge as the end of the process: read any NtO carefully. A Charge Certificate, Order for Recovery, removal, court, or enforcement wording needs urgent stage-specific checking.

Build a factual bundle: the full PCN and NtO, council photos/video, your own dated photos of signs/lines/bays, permit or payment proof, receipts, loading/delivery or appointment records, witness evidence where relevant, and a short timeline. Explain precisely what the evidence shows and why it matters to the allegation.

Official references (verify current rules and exact dates): https://www.gov.uk/parking-tickets/challenging-a-ticket and https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/civil-enforcement-of-parking-contraventions/guidance-for-local-authorities-on-enforcing-parking-restrictions
