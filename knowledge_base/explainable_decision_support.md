# Source label: explainable_decision_support.md

ParkPal should make every suggestion inspectable: show the notice wording or document that triggered it, the confidence level, the evidence still needed, the route it proposes, and the reason a deadline is estimated rather than explicit. Users should be able to correct extracted facts before relying on a draft.

Use confidence as a safety signal, not a claim of accuracy. Low confidence means "check the original notice"; it does not mean the notice is invalid. Recommendations should be phrased as possible actions, never guaranteed outcomes.

Good case assistance is transparent, user-controlled and evidence-led: it explains what is known, what is uncertain, what must be verified, and what should happen next. This is especially important for documents with legal or financial consequences.
