# Source label: ias_route.md

IAS-style escalation may be available after the parking operator has considered and rejected a first appeal, usually for eligible IPC operators linked to the applicable trade association scheme. Check the operator's membership, the rejection letter and current IAS instructions before using the route. Do not pay first without checking the effect on appeal eligibility.

The IAS says it considers the lawfulness of the charge and relevant Code issues on the evidence provided. It distinguishes standard and non-standard appeals, with different timing, cost and binding-effect rules. Use the exact current deadline and route stated by IAS and the operator; do not rely on a remembered timeframe.

Prepare the same evidence-led bundle: notice, rejection, photos of signs/markings/machines, receipts and payment records, permit or Blue Badge evidence where relevant, and a concise dated account. Mitigation alone may not answer the legal/factual issue, so connect each document to the point being made.

Official reference (verify current process): https://theias.org/before-you-appeal
