# Source label: popla_route.md

POPLA-style escalation may be available after an unsuccessful first appeal for eligible private-land Parking Charge Notices, normally involving a BPA operator. Check the rejection letter for the verification number, eligibility and deadline; do not assume a case is eligible just because it is a private parking charge.

Prepare the appeal before starting: state the issue plainly, attach the complete notice and rejection, then match each factual point to evidence. POPLA says it considers evidence from both the motorist and operator, and that its decision cannot later be reviewed. Keep the submission confirmation and monitor the case for the operator's evidence.

Evidence examples: clear sign/location photos for a signage point; payment confirmation, bank/app history and machine photos for a payment issue; original ANPR images plus receipts and a timeline for an ANPR or short-stay point; permit records for a permit issue. Do not submit a long generic template without evidence.

Official reference (verify current process): https://www.popla.co.uk/
