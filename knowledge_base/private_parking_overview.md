# Source label: private_parking_overview.md

Private Parking Charge Notices are not council PCNs. Read the notice to identify the operator, the alleged breach, the appeal method, and every stated deadline. Send the first appeal to the operator using its official channel, retain a copy of the submitted text and confirmation, and do not rely on a third-party payment or appeal link.

If the operator rejects the appeal, check its trade association and the rejection letter before selecting an independent route. GOV.UK says BPA operators normally use POPLA and IPC operators normally use IAS. The rejection letter is the source of truth for eligibility, codes and dates.

Notice to Keeper: preserve every page, the envelope if relevant, and the date received. Confirm whether the notice is addressed to the registered keeper and what facts it actually alleges. Do not add assumptions about who drove, why the vehicle was there, or the notice's legal effect; appeal only the facts and documents that can be supported.

Useful factual issues can include unclear or missing signage, a short stay where the terms or grace-period evidence supports it, a failed payment/app/machine, an ANPR timing error, a permit or payment not recognised, or wrong vehicle/location/date details. Check the exact allegation first. A grace-period point is not automatic: compare the operator's terms, on-site signs, and the entry/exit evidence.

Evidence to preserve: the complete notice, sign photographs (close and wide), payment/app screenshots, receipts, machine-fault photos, permit evidence, a dated timeline, and the operator's images/ANPR records where available.

Official reference (verify current route and deadlines): https://www.gov.uk/parking-tickets/challenging-a-ticket
